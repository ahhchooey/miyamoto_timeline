Miyamoto Shigeru

A pioneer of paths.

Miyamoto's Timeline

  • 1952 - Born in Sonobe, Japan on November 16
  • At an early age he was facinated with the natural area around his home and would spend much time exploring. This would eventually be influential in designing his adventure genre repertoire.
  • 1977-1984 - Beginnings at Nintendo
  • When Miyamoto first joined Nintendo, it was known for sellings cards, toys, and some games. He became the first artist for Nintendo and helped design the video game Sheriff. Further down the line he designed Donkey Kong, one of the first instances, if not the first, of a video game's story presceding the actual programming.
  • 1985-1989 - NES, Famicon, Super Mario Bros and The Legend of Zelda
  • It was during this time that Miyamoto had designed two of the most influential games in his lineup: Mario and Zelda. Both of which would grow to become two of the largest gaming franchises to ever exist.
  • 1990-2000 - SNES, N64
  • The world of gaming had began to expand into 3D and Miyamoto's Mari 64 and The Legend of Zelda: The Ocarina of Time would stand as revolutionary landmarks in terms of video game development.
  • 2000-2011 - Gamecube, Wii, DS
  • Miyamoto more Zelda and Mario games. He further expanded his development to create Metroid Prime and the Pikmin series.
  • 2011-present - Wii U, 3DS, Switch
  • Following the death of Nintendo President Iwata Satoru in 2015, Miyamoto was appointed acting Representative Director. He was relieved of the position when Kimishima Tatsumi assumed the role of Nintendo's president. Miyamoto then went to produce the next Mario game, Super Mario Odyssey.
